Wayfinding metaphor

  • • Urban planner Kevin Lynch first coined the term “wayfinding” to refer to systems that help people navigate through a space.

4 components of wayfinding

  1. 1. Orientation: Where am I am right now?
  2. 2. Route decisions: Can I find the way to where I want to go?
  3. 3. Mental mapping: Are my experiences consistent and understandable enough to know where I’ve been and to predict where I should go next?
  4. 4. Closure: Can I recognize that I have arrived in the right place?

Principles for wayfinding in websites

  1. 1. Paths: Create consistent and well-marked navigation paths.
  2. 2. Regions: Create a unique but related identity for each site region.
  3. 3. Nodes: Don’t confuse the user with too many choices on home and major menu pages.
  4. 4. Landmarks: Use consistent landmarks in site navigation and graphics to keep the user oriented.
